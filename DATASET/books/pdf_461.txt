e g studying global properties as degree distributions or on a microscopic level e g looking for frequent sub graphs or dense subgraphs We present an algorithm that performs an intermediate analysis of graphs by discovering patterns among microscopic and macroscopic properties over large networks Such patterns capture co variations between numerical vertex properties For in stance such a pattern in a co authorship graph could be the higher the number of publications in EGC the higher the centrality of the vertex within the graph Our contributions is threefold First this work is the first attempt to exploit local and topological properties over graphs Next we provide new insights into co variation pattern mining Finally we report a case study on a large real life co authorship network
