How ever it remains a challenging problem because of the high level of sparsity of the data in LBSN Moreover in this context the mobility behavior of the users is very hetero geneous ranging from urban to worldwide mobility In this paper we explore the impact of spatial clustering on the recommendation quality The proposed approach combines spatial clustering with users’ influences It is based on a Poisson factoriza tion model built on an implicit social network inferred from the geographical mobil ity patterns We conduct a comprehensive performance evaluation of our approach on the YFCC
